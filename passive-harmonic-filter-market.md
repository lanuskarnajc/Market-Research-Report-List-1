<p><p>The passive harmonic filter market primarily comprises tuned and de-tuned passive harmonic filters, each serving distinct purposes. Tuned passive harmonic filters are designed to target specific harmonic frequencies, effectively mitigating harmonic distortion in electrical systems. In contrast, de-tuned passive harmonic filters avoid resonance by shifting frequencies away from the system’s natural harmonics, providing broader frequency coverage. Both types contribute to improved power quality, enhanced system efficiency, and compliance with regulatory standards, catering to diverse industrial and commercial applications.</p></p>
