<p><p>Depth filtering is a filtration technique that utilizes a thick layer of granular or porous material to capture particles from a liquid or gas stream. Unlike surface filters, which capture particles on their surface, depth filters trap contaminants throughout the material, allowing for higher dirt-holding capacity and prolonged filter life. This method is widely used in various industries, including water treatment, pharmaceuticals, and food and beverage production.</p><p>The Depth Filtering Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 8.50% during the forecast period. Manufacturers are focusing on developing innovative depth filter media that enhance filtration efficiency, reduce waste, and lower operational costs. Moreover, advancements in filtration technology, such as the integration of smart systems for monitoring and maintenance, are also shaping the future of the market.
