<p><p>The shore power market consists of two main types: shoreside and shipside. Shoreside refers to the infrastructure and systems installed at ports or terminals that provide electrical power to vessels while docked, reducing emissions and reliance on onboard generators. Shipside, on the other hand, encompasses the equipment and technology on the vessel itself, allowing it to connect to the shoreside power supply. Together, these markets enhance sustainability in maritime operations by facilitating cleaner energy use during harbor stays.</p></p>
