<p><p>Hydrogel coatings for medical devices are designed to enhance biocompatibility and reduce friction, improving patient comfort and device performance. The market is divided into two main types: thermal heat cure and UV cure. </p><p>Thermal heat cure involves applying heat to polymerize the hydrogel, ensuring strong adhesion and durability. In contrast, UV cure utilizes ultraviolet light to initiate polymerization, allowing for rapid curing and precise control over the coating process. Both methods cater to diverse applications in medical devices, enhancing functionality and safety.</p></p>
