<p><ul><li>Intensity Autocorrelators</li><li>Interferometric Autocorrelators</li><li>Scanning Autocorrelators</li><li>Single-shot Autocorrelators</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Autocorrelators are devices used to analyze light pulses by measuring temporal coherence. Intensity autocorrelators assess pulse duration by detecting intensity variations. Interferometric autocorrelators utilize interference patterns to achieve high precision in temporal measurements. Scanning autocorrelators involve scanning techniques to capture pulse profiles over time, providing detailed temporal resolution. Single-shot autocorrelators offer immediate measurements of ultrafast pulses without the need for repetitive sampling, making them ideal for high-speed applications. Each type serves specific needs in optical research and industrial applications.</p></p>
