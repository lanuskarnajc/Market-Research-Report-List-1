<p><ul><li>Oropharyngeal Airway (OPA)</li><li>Nasopharyngeal Airway (NPA)</li><li>Endotracheal Airway (ETA)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The artificial airway market encompasses several key types: Oropharyngeal Airway (OPA), Nasopharyngeal Airway (NPA), and Endotracheal Airway (ETA). OPA is used to keep the airway open in unconscious patients by preventing tongue obstruction. NPA serves a similar purpose but is inserted through the nose, making it suitable for conscious patients. ETA provides direct access to the trachea for mechanical ventilation and is critical in emergency and surgical settings. Each type addresses specific airway management needs in healthcare.</p></p>
