<p><p>The acrylic fiber market is categorized into three main types: staple fiber, tows, and tops. Staple fiber is cut into short lengths for spinning into yarn, commonly used in textiles and apparel. Tows are long continuous strands used for making various products, including non-wovens and filling materials. Tops are processed fiber bundles prepared for spinning, enhancing yarn quality. Each type serves distinct applications, catering to industries such as fashion, home textiles, and industrial uses, contributing to the overall demand for acrylic fiber.</p></p>
